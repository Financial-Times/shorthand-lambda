Walk through some of the features available in Shorthand with this demo story.

Shorthand stories are built up of sections, with a number of different Section Types available for you to choose from. This story starts with a Title section, while this Text section delivers a short introduction.

You can add more sections by clicking the 'Add Section' button, in the bottom right-hand corner of the editor.

Here we have a Two Column Scrollmation section.

In Scrollmation-style sections like this, you can upload one or more images. As a reader scrolls through your story they'll see those images appear one after the other, in sequence.

Images in a Scrollmation section don't need to be closely related to one another, though some interesting effects can be achieved in desktop view if they are.

For instance: sequential frames taken from a video can give the effect of movement; maps can plot out a path relative to the text narrative, and charts can progressively reveal more information.

Your text can be further broken-up with inline images, which can be added by clicking 'Add Media' between paragraphs.

Your image caption can go here

Other options include YouTube video and SoundCloud audio, which can be added simply by entering the relevant URL.

Drag-and-drop sections to try different structures

Use the right-hand panel as your storyboard, dragging and dropping sections to change the order and experiment with different structures.

You can always delete sections later by clicking the red X on any section in the right-hand panel.

This is another Scrollmation section type called Background Scrollmation.

When you scroll through the example text you will see multiple images appear in sequence. This time, however, rather than appear in a column beside the scrolling text, the images sit full-screen in the background.

The length of your text and the number of images you use in the section will determine for how long each image will appear as your reader scrolls through the section.

Again, you can also add additional images, SoundCloud audio and YouTube video to the text column.

Once you've signed up to one of our pricing plans you'll also get access to our developer tools.

That includes custom CSS and Javascript, which you can add via the top menu, as well as the ability to add HTML between paragraphs in a text column, or as its own section.

Look for an embed feature in the other online tools you use, and you'll probably find that you can easily insert them into your Shorthand story.

Text Sections give your words focus.

The Text Section provides a simple text column with some white space, offering some visual breathing room to your narrative.

These columns don't have to be completely stark, however, and, just like in any other text column in Shorthand, can incorporate multiple types of media between paragraphs.

Lift your words off the page further by applying formatting. Highlight text and choose from the options available, including bold and italics.

Font and colour styles are set in a Theme, which can be applied across multiple stories for consistent branding. Click 'Themes' in the top menu to apply one of our standard themes, or send us your requirements to set up a branded theme with your own fonts and colours.

Give your readers a sense of control.

Background Scrollmation presents many opportunites for powerful visual storytelling, and one of our favourite is flip-book style animation.

When you upload images that are frames of a video, for example, you'll see the animation effect generated when you scroll through them, the speed of which is controlled by the reader.

This could be used to simulate any number of visual effects. Along with live-action movement, a section like this could just as easily appear to pan or zoom in on an image; overlay text, or slowly reveal detail.

Animation can bring a subject to life, add atmosphere, provide essential context. The possibilities are endless.

More images will result in a smoother movement – but keep an eye on performance as overloading browsers will impact on load.

Previewing your story

It's important to test your story at multiple screen resolutions and on multiple devices. The 'Preview' button at the top of the screen gives you a URL you can use to test out your story at any time. You can also click the dropdown next to the button to view the story across different devices in our preview simulator.

Different section types will react differently to different browser widths and types. Text Over Media sections gain their impact by filling the screen, but may be cropped by the browser in the process.

A Media section, by comparison (such as the one below), will scale images to fit within the screen - without being cropped, regardless of proportions.
